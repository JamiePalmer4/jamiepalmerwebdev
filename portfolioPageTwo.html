Queen Bee Café

The website Queen Bee Café was a project brief which was handed to me during my first year at college. This given piece of work incorporated my key skills that I had developed throughout the year and combined them into one final piece of work. The website featured a Home Page which gave an introduction to the cafe, with direct access to other important pages, along with Contact information and Opening Times for the business. Other pages included an About Us Page, which gave users information on the whereabouts of the cafe through a map location. A Shop Page was created to showcase the products that Queen Bee Café has to offer, with their menus for their brilliant food at their cafe. A Bee Keeping Page was created to give users an insight into how the honey is collected and crafted, with a short video of the process from the bees to the honey. Lastly, a Gallery Page was created to showcase the business, looking at their cafe, their products, and the most important aspect, the bees.

Key Features

Map Location

The brief that was given asked for a location pinpoint for the business, so users can easily locate the cafe on a map, instead of going elsewhere to get the details. This keeps the users engaged and have no reason to depart the website at such a quick notice.

Column Layout

A column layout was incorporated into the design in order to ensure that the website was responsive. This meant content on the website was displayed correctly and efficiently, causing no issues for users engaging with it.

Bee Keeping Video

The Database stores the records for all registered users on the website, along with the stock records. Access to the database can be found through the Admin Login form, which has a unique username and password in order to access both databases, and alter either of them.
